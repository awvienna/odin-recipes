Lasagna

Description

Originary from Italy, Lasagna is believed to be created by the Italians about 4389 years ago. (source: voices of my head)

This recipe will try to emulate what I've been eating when I go to all-you-can-eat italian restaurants. Here are the ingredients:

Ingredients

  • Lasagna pasta (it has to be the nonna one, otherwise it won't hold together)
  • Tomato sauce (the sauce that holds everything together)
  • Assorted complement, such as cheese, prosciutto or ham
  • Additional ingredients for extra flavour: groundbeef, cream cheese, scallions and fried garlic

Step by Step Instructions

  1. Cook ground beef or sausage in a pan, then add pasta sauce and simmer. Boil lasagna sheets until al dente, then drain!
  2. In a baking dish, layer sheets, meat sauce, and ricotta or cottage cheese. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella and Parmesan on top of each layer. Repeat until the dish is filled.
  3. Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for about 25-30 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 10-15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  4. Finally, cut it in square pieces and eat it while hot.
  5. Share it with friends!
